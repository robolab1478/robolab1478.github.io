Introduction

What are Plastics?

  • Synthetic goodness made from organic compounds
    • Derived from petrochemicals
    • Some are fully organic
  • Used in daily life
    • Cars (~20%)
    • Electronics (aesthetics / internal components)
    • Toys (for kids) (... and adults)
  • Yuge industry
    • $373 billion / year in production
    • Intermediary product

Water Bottle Production

  1. Dimethyl terephthalate and ethylene glycol react to form bis-(-hydroxyethyl) terephthalate (BHET) and methanol
    • Methanol boils off at reaction temperature (~220-260Â°C)
  2. BHET is further heated, where it undergoes a condensation reaction to form PET polymer

Environmental ramifications

  • Environmentally Safe Biodegradation
    • Nocardia bacterium with the help of an esterase enzyme
    • The Ideonella sakaiensis
      • 1 colony can degrade a bottle in about 6 weeks!
  • Acetaldehyde
    • Forms through mishandling of material (High temperatures, pressures, and extruder speeds)
  • Antimony
    • Trace amounts remain in plastic and can migrate out
      • Possible endocrine disruptor
  • Environmentally Unsafe Biodegradation
    • Typically in ocean water, the release of BPA and PS oligomer affect wildlife and potentially people
