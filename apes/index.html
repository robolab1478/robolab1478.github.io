Land, Water, CO2, pollution

  • Land - Mines resources. Requires clearing and grading. Causes deforestation and loss of biodiversity. 2.8 acres of land are needed to generate 1 gigawatt of energy using solar technology.
  • Water - Clearing and grading result in alteration of drainage channels which increases runoff and erosion. Chemical spills (for example, dust suppressants, dielectric) can contaminate water supplies. This can have an especially devastating effect on arid areas. However, after production, minimal water is used.
  • Carbon Dioxide - Mining resources and deforestation can increase carbon dioxide levels. Change in CO2 levels vary.
  • Pollution - Mining Resources and Land Development increases pollutants in the atmosphere. Pollution is produced based on materials required and geographic location. Amount of pollution varies

Net Energy

Net Energy is defined as "the ratio of energy required to release usable energy and the useful energy ."

Solar power has a net energy of 3:2

Time Frame

  • 1954 - Daryl Chapin, Calvin Fuller, and Gerald Pearson design a photovoltaic cell at bell labs, which was the first solar cell capable of generating enough electricity to power everyday equipment.
  • 1982 - The worldwide production of energy from solar energy exceeds 9.3 megawatts, just the next year in 1983, production almost doubles to 21.3 megawatts.
  • 1999 - After continued improvements, worldwide production using photovoltaic cells exceeds 1000 megawatts

Socio / Political / Humanitarian Conditions

  • SEIA (Solar Energy Industries Association) - agreed to a commitment toward social and environmental responsibility.
  • Social - Operating with respect to human rights and electrical safety; Working hard to reduce resource consumption and maximize energy efficiency. Additionally, requires workplace safety.
  • Political - Subsidize and continue to mandate only if produces the same amount of energy as fossil fuels and has less environmental damage.
  • Humanitarian - There's a lot we've done - Reduce fires, help improve study time in developing countries, become more environmentally friendly and continue to provide a a healthy electricity production solution (no toxins or socially dangerous materials)

Pros / Cons

Pros

  1. Indefinitely renewable
  2. Require little maintenance
  3. Has several different uses

Cons

  1. Cannot create electricity at night (intermittent)
  2. Requires large amounts of land area to be able produce energy for a community
  3. Low conversion efficiency
